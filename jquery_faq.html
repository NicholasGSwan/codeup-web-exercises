National Parks FAQ

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Michael T. Reynolds is the acting directer of the National Park Service.
What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The Department of the Interior oversees the NPS.
How many employees are in the National Park Service?
There are approximately 22,000 permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees, and 340,000 volunteers.
How old is the National Park System?
Woodrow Wilson signed the act creating the National Park Service in 1916, making it over 100 years old.
What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
It became the official NPS emblem on July 20, 1951, authorized by the Secretary of the Interior.
How many areas are in the National Park System?
There are 417 ares in the NPS.
What is the largest national park site? Smallest?
Largest: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, Alaska. (13.2 million acres.)
Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, Pennsylvania. (0.02 acres)
How many people visit the national parks?
In 2016 there were over 330 million recreational visitors to the national parks.
What is the most-visited national park?
Great Smoky Moutains NP was the most visited in 2015.
What is the National Park Service budget?
Fiscal Year 2017 enacted: $2.932 billion.

Yellowstone

  • Yellowstone spans 3 states: Idaho, Montanna, and Wyoming.
  • The park gets its name from the major river running through it, the Yellowstone River.
  • The highest peak in the park is Eagle Peak at 11,358 ft.
  • Yellowstone does not contain a federally designated wilderness.

Acadia

  • Acadia is located in Maine.
  • Acadia contains 125 miles of hiking trails and 45 miles of carriage roads.
  • The name comes from historical descriptions first used by fishermen and traders.
  • It is on Mount Desert Island, meaning it has no mountains.

Zion

  • Zion National Park is located in Utah.
  • Rockfalls are very common in Zion.
  • Only about 5 people have died falling from Angels Landing.
  • Trails are paved to prevent erosion.
